The concept

We all have a first and second space.
Where's your third?

1st
🏠

Home

Where you live, rest, and recharge. Your private world — family, comfort, routine. Essential, but not enough on its own.

2nd
💼

Work

Where you spend most of your waking hours. Structured, purposeful — but defined by obligation, not choice.

3rd
✨

Your third space

Not home. Not work. The place that's entirely yours.

The pub where they know your order. The Tuesday night class you actually look forward to. The room full of people who get it — and get you.

We're bringing that back.

The concept of the "third place" was coined by sociologist Ray Oldenburg in 1989. He argued that informal gathering spots — the local pub, the barbershop, the café — are the glue of community life. Today, those places are disappearing. My Third Space exists to find them, support them, and fill them with people.
